The Welfare State

EN

What is The Welfare State?

"It is a concept of government in which the state or a well-established network of social institutions plays a key role in the protection and promotion of the economic and social well-being of citizens."

Or with other words it is a system in which the government tries to help with social problems. That includes services like public education, health and transport.

First steps to The Welfare State

The idea for The Welfare State first came from the Royal Commision into the Operation of the Poor Laws 1832 which thought that the old law was subject to widespread abuse and promoted idleness and criminality. People's opinion began to change after reports showing that in massive industial cities, between 25% and 35% of the population were living below the poverty line. The introduction of welfare policies began in the 1910s. There were three main groups that were targeted: the young, the old and the working.

The Young

  • In 1906 schools were allowed to provide free school meals
  • In 1908 The Children and Young Pesons Act 1908 was introduced with a set of regulations that became known as the Children's Charter. It:
    • Imposed evere punishment for neglecting or treating children cruelly
    • Made selling cigaretes to children illegal
    • Made sending children for begging illegal
    • Set up separate juvenile courts which sent children guilty commiting a crime to bortsrals (a youth detention centres) instead of prison

The Old

  • In 1908 pensions were introduced for people over the age of 70

The Working

  • In 1909 Labour Exchanges were set up to help unemployed people find job
  • The National Insurance Act 1911 was passed and ensured free medical treatmernt, and sick pay of 10 shillings a week for 26 weeks. (Around 13 million workers were covered by this scheme)

Post-war Years

After the wars the Labour government was elected and as no surpise they set about creating the Welfare State as they were supporting the working class. The difference this time is that they were planning to complete it. The idea of the 'Welfare State' is to let the Central Government together with loacl authorities deal with the social problems and to take responsibility of fixing them. The priority was to maintain a high level of employment through the nationalisation of major industries. There were also the goals of providing efficient public services like: education, health and transport. After a bit of time with the new ideas Britain started to distribute the wealth to the population and came to the conclusion that both sexes should be equal. In 1970 The Equal Pay Act and The Sex Descrimination Act in 1975 were passed. These acts made discrimination of women in the workplace illegal. And almost immediately after that The Race Ralations Act was passed in 1976. which tried to stop the discrimination and racism that emerged in an increasingly meltiethnical society.
